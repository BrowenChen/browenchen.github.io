    • Music

    I have always been a lifelong musician, currently a freelance jazz-fusion musician in the Bay Area. I am a classicaly trained violinist from age 5, and have been a jazz drummer and percussionist throughout high school. I've performed and studied jazz in UC Berkeley's Dann Zinn Advanced Jazz Combo, Big Band, and Nu Jazz Collective, The California Jazz Conservatory, and my fusion funk group Constant Time . Notable mentors and teachers I had the opportunity to study with include Jeff Massanari, Kevin Wang, Luca Mantovanelli, Tim Lerch, Dann Zinn, Steve Erquiaga, Bob Ross, and Ted Moore. I was a featured guitar soloist in 2009 with San Diego Young Artist Symphony to play "Canon Rock" , the song which inspired me to begin the guitar. Throughout high school, I accumulated over 2,000,000 video views on YouTube and 6,000+ subscribers while producing music videos, currently now writing and producing for my Instagram, with 5,200+ followers. I dabble with MIDI Synth guitars and electronic music production & sound design as well. I have also taught 3 semesters of an Intro to Jazz Harmony course at Berkeley, as well as private individual instruction. I am currently enrolled in ensembles at The California Jazz Conservatory and gigging in the Bay.

    Jazz-Fusion

    I'm heavily interested in modern fusion - a genre that combines jazz harmony and theory with modern rock and pop elements. Interested in the neuroscience of jazz improvisation?



    Electronic Production

    I aim to create more EDM, Progressive House, Pop type tracks, eventually incorporating more fusion guitar and guitar synth ideas. Currently use Logic Pro X, Omnisphere, Serum, and Massive for production and sound design.




    Events & Contact

    Berkeley Computational Cognition Lab

    Built the "Cognitive Tools for Self Mastery" project individually as an undergraduate research assistant, led by P.h.d candidate Falk Lieder and Professor Tom Griffiths in Berkeley's Computational Cognition Lab, drawing on their published paper on the gamification of optimal decision making based on machine learning, A.I research, behavioral experiments, and mathematical modeling to understand procrastination and decision making. This Ruby on Rails full-stack web app is built for first round experiments, with it's full features, functions, and data tracking aims outlined in the design document for the semester long project.

    Tempo - Productive Time Waster

    Have free time? Want to be productive? Well here's the solution for you. Built with a team of 5 for a Berkeley software engineering semester course, Tempo allows users to optimize their free time. Pulls in various articles, videos, activities related to your personal interests that fit your specified time constraints, as well as the ability to create your own custom activities to do in the future. Built on a Rails, BackboneJS, PostGre stack, utilizing Youtube, Reddit, NYT API's, and full integration testing libraries. Create an account, try it out!
